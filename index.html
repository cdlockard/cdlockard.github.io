I am a PhD candidate at the Paul G. Allen School of Computer Science & Engineering at the University of Washington, where I am advised by Hannaneh Hajishirzi. I work on artificial intelligence and machine learning, with particular application to the understanding of human communications. My research focuses on developing methods to help computers understand and extract information from natural language sources such as newspapers and websites. While at UW, I have also been fortunate to work closely with scientists at Amazon's Product Graph team, led by Xin Luna Dong.

I hold a BA in English from Harvard University and an MA in Interdisciplinary Computer Science from Mills College. Prior to arriving in Seattle, I completed a stint with the Big Data Analytics & Machine Intelligence group at NASA Langley Research Center, taught at Peking University in Beijing, and did some time in the business world.
